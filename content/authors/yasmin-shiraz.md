Yasmin Shiraz has spent decades at the intersection of storytelling, social commentary, and cultural advocacy. As the founder of The Yasmin Breakdown, she applies a sociological and criminological lens to the stories mainstream media skims past — breaking down the power dynamics, historical patterns, and human consequences behind the headlines in hip hop, sports, Black history, and popular culture.

A bestselling author and American Library Award winner, Yasmin's work spans fiction, nonfiction, and cultural analysis. She has written for Cold Case Files and founded Mad Rhythms, a hip hop magazine that amplified Black voices at the intersection of music and social justice.

Her analysis is rooted in lived experience, academic rigor, and a commitment to making complex sociological ideas accessible to everyday audiences. Whether she is dissecting a WNBA officiating controversy, contextualizing a Supreme Court ruling, or unpacking the sociology of Black fatherhood, Yasmin brings the same precision and passion that has defined her career.

The Yasmin Breakdown exists because these conversations matter — and because the people most affected by these stories deserve analysis that respects their intelligence.
